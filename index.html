ABOUT “DEADLINING”:

As neighborhoods transform, Black female bodies become both targets and warnings.

Their murders are simultaneously rising in number yet falling in priority.

GEOGRAPHY OF INVISIBILITY:

The highest documented case density occurred along Suitland Parkway, where a new era of redevelopment is transforming a corridor long shaped by displacement.

THE FINDING:

DOCUMENTED CASES CLUSTER WITHIN LIMINAL ZONES OF URBAN “REDEVELOPMENT” & JURISDICTIONAL INVISIBILITY.

WHY WE EXIST:

To close critical gaps in missing and murdered persons data by creating a publicly accessible forensic database that supports investigations and advances geospatial research into the structural conditions that shape victimization.
